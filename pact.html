The Thrilling Rivalry

The rivalry between the Audi Quattro and the Lancia 037 reached its peak during the 1983 and 1984 WRC seasons. The Quattro's all-wheel-drive system allowed it to excel on slippery and gravelly terrains, whereas the 037's lightweight design and rear-wheel-drive configuration made it nimble and agile on tarmac stages.
During the 1983 season, the Audi Quattro, driven by legendary names like Michèle Mouton and Stig Blomqvist, showcased its dominance on snow and gravel stages, securing victories in events like the Rally of Portugal and the Rally of New Zealand. On the other hand, the Lancia 037, piloted by the skilled pair of Walter Röhrl and Markku Alén, demonstrated its prowess on tarmac rallies, including victories at the Rallye Sanremo and the Tour de Corse.

Impact on Rally Racing

The thrilling rivalry between the Audi Quattro and the Lancia 037 had a profound impact on rally racing as a whole. The competition sparked an arms race of innovation and technology in the WRC, as manufacturers sought to outdo one another in the quest for rally supremacy.
Audi's introduction of all-wheel-drive technology in the Quattro permanently changed the landscape of rally racing. The success of the Quattro not only established Audi as a dominant force in the WRC but also influenced the design and engineering of rally cars for years to come. The all-wheel-drive revolutionized rally racing and became a standard feature in modern rally cars.
On the other hand, the Lancia 037 showcased the potential of rear-wheel-drive cars in the sport. Its agility and performance on tarmac stages demonstrated that there was more than one way to excel in rally racing. Although the rear-wheel-drive layout gradually faded in the WRC, it remains a cherished part of rally history and continues to be celebrated by motorsport enthusiasts.
